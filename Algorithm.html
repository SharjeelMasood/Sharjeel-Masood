What Is an Algorithm?

An algorithm is a step-by-step method used to solve a problem or complete a task. It is not limited to computers — humans use algorithms in daily life without realizing it. From following a recipe to planning a journey, algorithms are everywhere.

Why Algorithms Matter

Algorithms bring structure to problem-solving. Instead of guessing, they provide a clear path from start to finish. This makes tasks more efficient, predictable, and easier to improve.

A good algorithm reduces confusion and increases clarity.

Algorithms in Daily Life

When you follow steps to cook food, solve a math problem, or organize your day, you are using an algorithm. The same logic applies when technology processes information.

Characteristics of a Good Algorithm

  • Clear and understandable steps
  • Logical sequence
  • Defined start and end
  • Efficient use of time and resources
  • Easy to modify or improve

Thinking in Algorithms

Learning to think algorithmically improves decision-making skills. It teaches how to break complex problems into smaller, manageable parts. This mindset is valuable in every technical and non-technical field.

Key Takeaway

Algorithms are not just technical concepts — they are a way of thinking. Understanding them helps you solve problems logically, make better decisions, and approach challenges with confidence.
